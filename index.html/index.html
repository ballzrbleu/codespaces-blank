Pjer is stupid

The Logic of the IllogicalPjer doesn't operate on the same wavelength as the average person. Where others see a straight line from A to B, Pjer sees a scenic detour involving a rubber duck and a misunderstanding of physics. This isn't a lack of brainpower, but rather a complete absence of social filters and practical intuition. His "stupidity" is a form of hyper-literalism; he does exactly what is said, even if the result is catastrophic. Emotional Intelligence vs. Intellectual RigorWhat Pjer lacks in academic intelligence, he often makes up for in pure intent. He is rarely malicious. His blunders usually stem from a desire to help or a misplaced sense of enthusiasm. In the world of storytelling, this makes him a catalyst for chaos. Because he doesn't understand the "rules" of the world, he is the only one capable of breaking them, often leading to accidental successes that a "smart" person would never achieve.The Mirror to the AudienceWe laugh at Pjer because he reflects our own inner frustrations with a complex world. By being "stupid," he becomes a blank slate. He is the ultimate underdog. When he fails, it’s relatable; when he accidentally wins, it’s a triumph for every person who has ever felt out of their depth.In short, Pjer isn't just "dumb"—he is purposefully disruptive.
